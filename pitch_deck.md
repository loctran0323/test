## Slide 1 - The Problem

- Millions of elderly, disabled, and homebound Americans struggle to access reliable transportation.
- Missing transportation often means missing:
  - doctor appointments
  - grocery trips
  - pharmacy pickups
  - social and community activities
- Existing options are often not built around accessibility, reliability, or hands-on support.

**Core question:** How do we provide transportation that is reliable, affordable, and genuinely accessible?

**Visual idea:** Photo or icon set showing doctor visit, grocery store, wheelchair access, and home pickup.

---

## Slide 2 - Why This Matters

- Transportation is not just a convenience issue; it is a healthcare and quality-of-life issue.
- In 2022, **14.4% of U.S. adults with disabilities** reported lacking reliable transportation for daily living, versus **4.9%** of adults without disabilities.  
  *Source: CDC / National Health Interview Survey*
- Transportation barriers are linked to delayed care, missed appointments, and worse health outcomes.
- Families and caregivers are often unavailable to provide frequent rides.

**Takeaway:** Mobility gaps create both human costs and a large unmet market need.

---

## Slide 3 - Current Market Gap

### Existing options do not fully solve the problem

**Uber / Lyft / taxis**
- Convenient, but not designed for elderly care
- Limited accessibility support
- Drivers are not trained for assisted pickup and drop-off

**Paratransit services**
- Better accessibility, but often:
  - require advance reservations
  - involve long wait windows
  - can be slow and operationally rigid

**Gap in the market:** On-demand convenience + specialized accessibility service.

---

## Slide 4 - Our Solution

### A specialized ride-hailing and scheduling platform

Users can request rides through:
- mobile app
- website
- desktop/computer portal

Trip request includes:
- pickup and drop-off location
- desired arrival time
- accessibility needs
- support preferences

Examples of accommodations:
- wheelchair-accessible vehicles
- ramp/lift-equipped vans
- door-to-door or door-through-door assistance
- extra boarding time
- caregiver coordination

**Value proposition:** Uber-like convenience with paratransit-level accommodation.

---

## Slide 5 - What Makes It Disruptive

This is a **Schumpeter-style disruptive innovation** because it combines:

1. **The speed and convenience of ridesharing**
2. **The accessibility standards of paratransit**
3. **A care-centered experience for vulnerable riders**

Instead of forcing users to choose between convenience and accommodation, we deliver both in one platform.

**Differentiator:** We are not just moving passengers; we are providing dependable mobility for people with specialized needs.

---

## Slide 6 - Where AI Creates an Advantage

AI is the operating layer that makes this model scalable.

### AI applications

- **Ride matching:** pair riders with the right driver, vehicle, and accommodation level
- **Arrival-time prediction:** forecast delays and improve on-time performance
- **Route optimization:** minimize idle time and improve fleet utilization
- **Demand forecasting:** predict peak periods by location, facility, and time of day
- **Support recommendation engine:** suggest the best ride setup based on rider history

**Why this matters:** Accessibility service is expensive to operate unless dispatch, routing, and scheduling are optimized.

## Slide 8 - Business Model

- Generate revenue through:
  - ride fees

  - institutional subscriptions

### Phase 2: Platform expansion

- Build proprietary rider-facing app and scheduling system
- Expand driver and vehicle network

### Phase 3: Scaled network

## Slide 10 - Go-to-Market Strategy

### Step 1: Start local

- Launch in one region with a high concentration of:
  - senior communities
  - hospitals
  - recurring care trips

### Step 2: Build trust

- Focus on reliability, safety, and caregiver-friendly service
- Track metrics such as:
  - on-time arrival rate
  - missed-trip reduction
  - repeat rider usage
  - customer satisfaction

### Step 3: Expand through partnerships

- Use B2B relationships to lower customer acquisition cost
- Turn healthcare institutions into repeat-demand anchors

---

## Slide 11 - Risks and Challenges

- Vehicle accessibility raises operating complexity
- Driver training and service quality must be tightly managed
- Reliability must be high from day one
- Regulatory and insurance requirements may vary by state
- Building trust with vulnerable users is essential

**Our response:** Start with partnerships, focus on a narrow launch market, and use AI to improve dispatch efficiency before scaling.
